About the Project

Building Security Standards for the Mind

The ONI Framework is an open research initiative developing neurosecurity standards for brain-computer interfaces — before the industry needs them retroactively.

The Researcher

KQ

Kevin Qi

Neurosecurity Researcher & Framework Architect

Working at the intersection of neuroscience, quantum security, and AI ethics. Kevin created the ONI Framework to address a gap no one was filling: a comprehensive, layered security model purpose-built for brain-computer interfaces. His work draws from the OSI networking model, quantum information theory, and neuroethics to define how neural data should be protected at every layer — from ion channels to identity.

Mission & Vision

“The mind is the last frontier. We're building the security standards to protect it.”

Brain-computer interfaces are being implanted in humans today — with no universal security standard. Companies are racing to ship neural hardware while fundamental questions about data sovereignty, cognitive privacy, and adversarial resilience remain unanswered.

The ONI Framework exists to change that. We are developing a ground-up, 14-layer reference architecture for neurosecurity — extending the principles of OSI networking into biological territory. Our goal: ensure that by the time BCIs reach mass adoption, the security standards are already in place.

Research Philosophy

Three principles guide every decision in this project:

Transparency

Built through documented Human-AI collaboration. Every contribution is audited. AI assistance is disclosed, not hidden. We maintain a public transparency statement and cognitive boundary log.

Open Source

All framework documents, code, and visualizations are publicly available under Apache 2.0. Security through obscurity is not security — especially when the attack surface is the human brain.

Neuroethics-First

Ethics is not a chapter at the end — it is baked into every layer. The framework maps to UNESCO neuroethics principles and includes informed consent, pediatric protections, and accessibility by design.
